# Sequencing & Timing

Use these terms to describe how multiple animation moments are ordered, delayed, and coordinated.

- **Keyframes**: Defined points in an animation timeline, such as `0%`, `50%`, and `100%`.
- **Interpolation / Tween**: The generated in-between frames that make motion feel continuous between two states.
- **Stagger**: A sequence where related elements animate one after another with a small delay.
- **Orchestration**: Coordinating several animation parts so they read as one intentional motion.
- **Delay**: The waiting time before an animation begins.
- **Duration**: The amount of time an animation takes to play.
- **Fill mode**: Whether an element keeps styles from the first or last animation frame before or after playback, such as `forwards`.
- **Stepped animation**: An animation divided into discrete jumps instead of smooth continuous movement.
